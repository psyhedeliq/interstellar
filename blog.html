Resupply Mission

Written by Jeff on 04/20

Packed with about two tons of cargo and science, the InterStellar spacecraft departed the International Space Station on Sunday, January 13.

Filled with more than 5,600 pounds of supplies and payloads, the cargo launched aboard a rocket on December 5, 2018 from Cape Canaveral Air Force Station in Florida, and arrived at the space station on December 8.The spacecraft flown on this mission previously visited the space station during SpaceX’s CRS-10 mission in February 2017.

Nusantra Satu Mission

Written by Karen on 05/22

On Thursday, February 21 at 8:45 p.m. EST, InterStellar launched the Nusantara Satu satellite from Space Launch Complex 40 (SLC-40) at Cape Canaveral Air Force Station, Florida.

The roket's first stage for the Nusantara Satu mission previously supported the Iridium-7 mission in July 2018 and the SAOCOM 1A mission in October 2018. Following stage separation, SpaceX landed Falcon 9’s first stage on the “Of Course I Still Love You” droneship, which was stationed in the Atlantic Ocean.

Iridium-8 Mission

Written by Harry on 07/10

On Friday, January 11 at 7:31 a.m. PST, 15:31 UTC, InterStellar successfully launched the eighth and final set of satellites in a series of 75 total satellites for Iridium’s next generation global satellite constellation, Iridium NEXT.

Following stage separation, the first stage of the rocket successfully landed on the “Just Read the Instructions” droneship stationed in the Pacific Ocean. The rocket's first stage for the Iridium-8 mission previously supported the Telstar 18 VANTAGE mission in September 2018.
